Types of Events We Host

Cultural Festivals

Celebrate major Hindu festivals like Diwali, Holi, Navratri, and Ganesh Chaturthi with traditional food, music, dance, and community bonding.

Educational Workshops

Learn about Hindu philosophy, Sanskrit language, yoga techniques, meditation practices, and cultural traditions from experienced practitioners.

Community Service

Participate in food drives, blood donation camps, environmental cleanups, and volunteer work at local temples and community centers.

Social Gatherings

Enjoy informal meetups, game nights, movie screenings, potluck dinners, and networking events to build lasting friendships.

Spiritual Discussions

Join thoughtful discussions about Hindu texts, philosophy, spirituality, and how ancient wisdom applies to modern life.

Cultural Performances

Showcase and enjoy classical Indian dance, music, drama, and artistic performances by talented community members.

Recent Past Events

Fall 2024 Highlights

  • Diwali Festival (November 2024): Over 200 attendees celebrated the Festival of Lights with traditional performances, rangoli competition, and authentic Indian cuisine
  • Karwa Chauth Celebration (October 2024): A beautiful evening celebrating the bond between couples with traditional ceremonies and community feast
  • Navratri Dance Night (October 2024): Nine nights of energetic Garba and Dandiya dancing with live music and colorful traditional attire
  • Ganesh Chaturthi (September 2024): Sacred celebration honoring Lord Ganesha with prayers, modak preparation, and cultural programs

Spring 2024 Highlights

  • Holi Festival (March 2024): Vibrant celebration of colors with organic gulal, traditional sweets, and joyful music
  • Ram Navami (April 2024): Spiritual celebration honoring Lord Rama with devotional singing and community prasad
  • Earth Day Service (April 2024): Environmental cleanup drive at local parks demonstrating our commitment to Mother Earth
  • Khel Utsav Sports Day (May 2024): Friendly sports competition promoting fitness, teamwork, and healthy competition
